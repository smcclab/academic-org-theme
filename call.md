TechArts 2025 invites contributions across three tracks. All contributions are reviewed through the same submission site and follow the same timeline; submission templates differ by track.

<div class="info-box" markdown="1">
**Important dates** — Submissions open **1 May 2025**. Submissions close **15 July 2025**. Notifications **20 August 2025**. Camera-ready **15 September 2025**. Conference **16–17 October 2025**.
</div>

## Papers track

The Papers track publishes original research on creative computing, interactive systems, and adjacent areas. Two paper formats are accepted:

- **Long papers** (up to 8 pages) — mature work with complete methodology and evaluation. Presented as 15-minute talks.
- **Short papers, posters and demos** (up to 4 pages) — early-stage work, novel prototypes, and case studies. Presented as 5–8 minute talks, posters, or live demos depending on what best suits the contribution.

All papers are peer-reviewed and appear in the open-access proceedings.

## Artworks track

The Artworks track welcomes completed works for presentation at the conference venue in two formats:

- **Installations** — works exhibited across the full duration of the conference in the Gallery Hall.
- **Performances** — scored or improvised works presented at the Performance Night in the Black Box Theatre.

A single work may be proposed as both an installation and a performance; see [Feedback Garden](/proceedings/41.html) for an example of this hybrid format.

## Workshops track

The Workshops track runs hands-on half-day sessions in parallel with the conference program. Proposals should describe the intended audience, prerequisites, materials required, and the practical outcome participants will take away.

<div class="warning-box" markdown="1">
Workshop proposals are capped at **two pages** and must include a clear logistics plan. Venue-provided equipment is limited; check the [venue guide](/location/) before proposing hardware-heavy workshops.
</div>

## Reviewing

All tracks use double-anonymous peer review with at least three reviewers per submission. Meta-reviews are provided for disagreements. The review form is published on the submission site.
